# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FX Logistics serves shipment recipients, senders, and operations users who need fast, trustworthy freight visibility. Public users arrive with a tracking number and want a live answer without creating an account. Business users need to book shipments, monitor active freight, review exceptions, and keep delivery commitments visible.

## Product Purpose

The product turns a shipment tracking site into a full logistics platform. Success means tracking stays instantly accessible from every surface, booking is structured enough to reduce errors, and authenticated users can understand shipment health without digging through scattered tools.

## Brand Personality

Precise, accountable, direct. The interface should feel like logistics infrastructure: calm under pressure, clear about status, and confident without sounding like generic software marketing.

## Anti-references

Avoid the old Obriship visual identity, acid-yellow startup accents, decorative globe animation, generic tech-SaaS copy, dead icon buttons, and oversized marketing ornament. The product should not feel like a crypto dashboard, a soft pastel SaaS landing page, or a decorative travel site.

## Design Principles

1. Keep tracking in reach: the primary product action should remain available in the navigation and on every major workflow surface.
2. Make status legible before it is beautiful: shipment state, dates, exceptions, and next actions must scan quickly.
3. Earn emphasis: use strong color only for primary actions, active states, critical highlights, and meaningful status.
4. Design for interrupted work: forms, dashboards, and tracking pages should tolerate partial attention and mobile use.
5. Prefer operational specificity: copy should name the freight task, status, or outcome instead of leaning on abstract platform language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible interactions. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid color-only status communication, keep labels explicit on forms, and ensure the mobile experience works from a 360px viewport.
